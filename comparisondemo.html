Threat Model Attacker has breached one application-layer database. No prior knowledge of any patient record. What can they recover?

Direct Hashing Pipeline

hashing.db — single database, all fields hashed with SHA-256

Vulnerable
Data flow & storage
What the attacker sees
Hashes mrn_hash, first_name_hash, last_name_hash, dob_hash, ssn_hash crackable
Every field is a SHA-256 hash of the original value. SSNs are low-entropy — the constrained synthetic SSN format yields 90 million candidates enumerable in seconds with no prior knowledge. No patient_id means no direct record linkage, but the hash itself is the vulnerability. Once any field is recovered the attacker can cross-reference against public records.

Tokenization Pipeline

token_vault.db — CSPRNG tokens alongside plaintext originals, single database

Vault-Dependent
Data flow & storage
What the attacker sees
Tokens mrn_token, first_name_token, last_name_token, dob_token, ssn_token not crackable
Plaintext patient_id, mrn_original, first_name_original, last_name_original, dob_original, ssn_original plaintext
CSPRNG tokens are not crackable — 256 bits of entropy with no structural relationship to the original value. However, the vault stores plaintext originals alongside every token. A breach of token_vault.db directly exposes all patient data with no cryptographic attack required. The attacker simply reads the _original columns. Security relies entirely on vault isolation.

Layered Architecture Pipeline

layered_vault1.db · layered_link.db · layered_vault2.db — three physically isolated databases

Recommended

What the attacker sees — per database breach
layered_vault1.db
Plaintext last_name plaintext
Hashes last4_ssn_hash crackable (4 digits)
Tokens last_name_token, last4_ssn_token not crackable
layered_link.db
Plaintext dob plaintext
Hashes last4_ssn_hash crackable (4 digits)
Tokens last4_ssn_token, patient_id_token not crackable
layered_vault2.db
Plaintext patient_id, first_name, ssn, last4_ssn, mrn plaintext
Tokens patient_id_token, first_name_token, mrn_token not crackable
Patient data is deliberately distributed across three physically isolated databases. No single breach yields a complete or immediately exploitable record. An attacker must simultaneously compromise all three databases and successfully traverse the full token chain — Vault 1 to Link DB to Vault 2 — to reconstruct a full patient identity. Each database in isolation contains only a fragment of the picture, connected to the others exclusively through opaque CSPRNG tokens that carry no structural relationship to any patient identifier.
